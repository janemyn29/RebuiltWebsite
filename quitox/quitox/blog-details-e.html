Vietnamese engineer invents chocolate heating machine

June 17, 2021 / by amazing_truong

Mr. Nguyen Hong Huy (32 years old), researched a chocolate heating machine with a capacity of 40 kg per hour, half the price of an imported machine.

Heating is the most important and costly step in the chocolate production process from cocoa beans. Before heating, cocoa beans will go through stages such as roasting, tearing, crushing, pressing butter...

KS Nguyen Hong Huy said that in order for chocolate bars to be smooth and shiny, the heating stage plays a decisive role. The heaters will adjust the temperature differently (from about 30 - 50 degrees Celsius) to help the ingredients including chocolate butter, sugar, cocoa beans blend together. If the heating process is not correct when pouring the mold, it will cause the chocolate bars to float crystals of butter and sugar on the surface, affecting the aesthetics and quality of the product. "When eating, the chocolate must melt in the mouth, if there are crystals left, the product is assessed as poor quality, " Huy said.

Chocolate heating machine researched by Mr. Huy.

At the heating stage, many domestic chocolate processing enterprises use imported machines originating from the US, Germany, China... with high cost, about 400-500 million VND for a capacity of 40 kg or more. From this fact, in 2019, Huy invented a completely domestic product with equivalent quality but cost more than 200 million VND.

The machine is designed with a capacity of 40 kg per hour, consisting of four main components: temperature control system, grinding knife system, vertical pump unit and control unit. When putting ingredients including chocolate butter, sugar, cocoa beans into the mixing tray, the grinding knife system will work in a circle to help mix the ingredients together. Along with that, the temperature control system will increase and decrease the heat and cold appropriately so that the ingredients blend together best. According to Huy, the temperature adjustment process must be flexible so that the chocolate does not burn when the temperature is high and crystallizes when the temperature is low. After the heating process, the chocolate mixture is poured into the mold, cooled, and then removed from the mold.

The heater can still produce a defective product with a rate of about 10%, ie the chocolate bar is crystallized. Then the products continue to be put into the tray, reheated according to the old process to fix the error. At present, the product has completed the commercial version, prepared the industrial design registration procedures and is ready to be transferred to businesses in need.

"If the product is commercialized, it will help cocoa product manufacturers expand production at a lower cost," Huy said.

Huy shared that he spent more than a year tinkering with research and testing products at a mechanical workshop in Thu Dau Mot, Binh Duong. As an engineer in the automotive industry, with no expertise in refrigeration technology, he researched documents and purchased the heating and cooling systems of old equipment and tested many times to assess suitability. . In February 2020, after completing the product, he shared the product with the community of international chocolate manufacturers. After that, Huy accepted an offer to buy a machine from a number of businesses from India, Cameroon...

Mr. Arnold Epanty, CEO of Mandop Foods - a food processing enterprise in Cameroon, said that for chocolate heaters, the only competitive factor right now is the price because there are many businesses around the world supplying them. with many different varieties. He expressed his impression of Huy's products, demonstrating the investment in technology, because the heating structure is inherently difficult and complicated to do.

He said that if Huy's machine has a lower price than other types of machines today and equivalent quality, there is a great potential for market development in other countries, with a high probability of success. "If Vietnamese enterprises meet these requirements, we are ready to buy to expand production," Mr. Arnold Epanty shared on the forum.
